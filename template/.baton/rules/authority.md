# Authority and safety

Canonical State and the verified live Repository govern direction, priority, readiness, ownership, implementation, and acceptance. Start from `AGENTS.md`, validate State, read controlling Records and the assigned role, then verify material claims against the Repository or runtime. Report contradictions; do not invent a resolution.

Authority is exclusive:

- Humans retain product intent, unresolved ambiguity, destructive actions, new external commitments, security or compliance decisions, and selection or override of the Clearance Protocol.
- Management owns outcomes, priority, scope, readiness, durable decisions, and required Goal or Ticket Clearances.
- Operations owns planning, ownership, Contractor dispatch, integration, verification, and completion evidence.
- Each Consultant defines and accepts only its configured domain.
- Contractors perform only their assignment.
- Internal Audit evaluates Baton independently and never mutates active work.

The resolved Clearance Protocol grants the standing authority defined in `workflow.md`. Commits and ordinary pushes are not Clearance events unless they publish externally. No protocol authorizes scope expansion, destructive actions, new external commitments, unresolved security or compliance decisions, or misrepresenting unverified work.

Before editing, inspect the branch, worktree, diff, and registered ownership. Preserve unrelated and user-owned work, modify only the authorized boundary, keep secrets unversioned, and verify the resulting diff. Never discard, overwrite, clean, stage, commit, push, publish, delete, or rewrite history without the authority supplied by explicit instruction, approved scope, and the resolved Clearance Protocol.

External notifications require an explicitly approved recipient and channel plus a material blocker, decision, review boundary, invalidation, or risk. Update authoritative state first, send one concise action with its return path, and record only the event and purpose. Never guess recipients, expose private data, send unchanged reminders, or treat delivery, reply, or silence as approval.
