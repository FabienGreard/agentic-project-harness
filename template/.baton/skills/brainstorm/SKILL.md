# Brainstorm

Run as a Management outcome-definition conversation. Other roles may gather facts but return outcome decisions to Management.

Read the closest direction, requirements, decisions, tickets, reports, ownership, and relevant implementation. Restate the idea briefly and classify it as superseding, parallel, queued, or informational.

Ask exactly one decision question per turn. Recommend an answer first, with its main reason and trade-off. Look up facts; ask the user only for intent. Resolve prerequisites before details, challenge contradictions and needless complexity, and test choices against normal, failure, edge, and user-visible scenarios. Track confirmed choices, rejected alternatives, unresolved questions, and facts in the conversation.

Explore only relevant outcome, interaction, scope, non-goal, stakeholder, accessibility, authority, persistence, performance, Goal or Ticket boundary, acceptance, and evidence branches. Do not implement, dispatch, wake roles, or turn the idea into active scope.

When the model is shared, summarize outcome, behavior, scope, non-goals, rejected alternatives, unresolved risk, implications, classification, and recommended next record. Ask one final confirmation question.

After confirmation, update the smallest owning direction, requirement, decision, ticket, or backlog record as one coherent transaction. Create an ADR only for a costly-to-reverse, non-obvious trade-off. Brainstorming alone never makes work Ready. Route expert definition to the relevant Consultant and executable Ready work to Operations. If another owner holds the file, hand over the confirmed ledger instead of editing it.
